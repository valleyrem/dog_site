About

Welcome!

This site is created for fun and gives brief information about dog breeds, categorized by American Kennel Club (AKC) - one of the oldest and most respected dog breed registries in the world (founded in 1884). AKC sets breed standards, organizes shows and events, and offers various resources for dog owners.

So, currently, AKC identifies the following breed groups:

  • Sporting Group: Breeds for hunting and retrieving, known for agility and tracking abilities.
  • Hound Group: Breeds for hunting by sight and scent, including sighthounds and scent hounds.
  • Working Group: Breeds used for guarding, sledding, and rescues, known for strength and intelligence.
  • Terrier Group: Energetic breeds originally for hunting and pest control.
  • Toy Group: Small companion breeds, perfect for city living.
  • Non-Sporting Group: Diverse breeds with unique traits and personalities.
  • Herding Group: Breeds skilled in managing livestock, intelligent and energetic.
  • Miscellaneous Class: Breeds on their way to full AKC recognition.
  • Foundation Stock Service: Breeds in the early stages of AKC recognition.

Breeds: The list of breeds here isn’t complete. If you want to help and add missing breeds, please register and check the list to make sure the breed isn’t already included. If you need more details, please visit the official AKC website to find more information about any breed.
