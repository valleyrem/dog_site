About Woof Dogs

Welcome

This site offers a brief overview of dog breeds, categorized by American Kennel Club (AKC).

It is created for fun and gives brief information about dog breeds. If you need more details, please visit the official American Kennel Club (AKC) website to find comprehensive information about dog breed.

Breed Listings: The list of breeds here isn’t complete. If you want to help add missing breeds, please register and check both the AKC list and this site list to make sure the breed isn’t already included.

Our Beloved Pets: Love animals? Whether it's a dog, cat, bird, or any other cherished pet, share their details or pictures here. You can fill out the New Article form to add your pet to the Our Beloved Pets category.

Currently, AKC identifies the following breed groups:

  • Sporting Group: Breeds for hunting and retrieving, known for agility and tracking abilities.
  • Hound Group: Breeds for hunting by sight and scent, including sighthounds and scent hounds.
  • Working Group: Breeds used for guarding, sledding, and rescues, known for strength and intelligence.
  • Terrier Group: Energetic breeds originally for hunting and pest control.
  • Toy Group: Small companion breeds, perfect for city living.
  • Non-Sporting Group: Diverse breeds with unique traits and personalities.
  • Herding Group: Breeds skilled in managing livestock, intelligent and energetic.
  • Miscellaneous Class: Breeds on their way to full AKC recognition.
  • Foundation Stock Service: Breeds in the early stages of AKC recognition.

Explore our breed profiles to find the perfect dog for you, whether you're seeking a loyal companion, a working dog, or specific traits.
